# AMD Threadripper Series CPUs: Price Differences and Specs

## Overview

AMD's Threadripper series CPUs are high-end desktop processors designed for users who demand exceptional performance for tasks such as 3D rendering, video editing, scientific simulations, and virtualization. These CPUs are based on the Zen micro-architecture and support DDR4 memory. The latest models, including the 3990X, 3970X, and 3960X, offer varying core counts and thread counts, making them suitable for different workloads and budgets.

## Key Models

### AMD Ryzen Threadripper 3990X
- **Core/Thread Count:** 64 cores, 128 threads
- **Price:** $4,355
- **Specs:** The 3990X is the top-of-the-line model, offering the highest core and thread count. It's ideal for users who need maximum processing power for demanding tasks.

### AMD Ryzen Threadripper 3970X
- **Core/Thread Count:** 32 cores, 64 threads
- **Price:** $1,939.88
- **Specs:** The 3970X is a powerful CPU with half the core and thread count of the 3990X, making it a more affordable option for users who don't need the full power of the 3990X.

### AMD Ryzen Threadripper 3960X
- **Core/Thread Count:** 24 cores, 48 threads
- **Price:** $1,409.99
- **Specs:** The 3960X is a mid-range option, offering a balance between performance and cost. It's suitable for users who need a powerful CPU without breaking the bank.

## Comparison

### Core and Thread Count
- The 3990X offers twice the core and thread count of the 3970X and 3960X, making it the most powerful option.
- The 3970X and 3960X offer a more balanced performance-to-price ratio, with the 3970X being a better value for its price compared to the 3960X.

### Memory Support
- All Threadripper CPUs support DDR4 memory, with improved memory topology in the 3rd generation models.
- The 3990X, 3970X, and 3960X all have direct memory channels, ensuring optimal memory performance.

### PCIe Lanes
- All Threadripper CPUs offer a large number of PCIe lanes, which is crucial for users who need to connect multiple high-speed devices.

### Thermal Design Power (TDP)
- The 3990X has a TDP of 280W, while the 3970X and 3960X have TDPs of 250W and 250W, respectively.
- The higher TDP of the 3990X reflects its higher power consumption and performance.

### Cost per Thread
